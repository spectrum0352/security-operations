# Communication During a Cybersecurity Incident

Effective communication is essential during a cybersecurity incident. Timely, accurate, and secure communication helps coordinate response efforts, minimizes confusion, satisfies legal and regulatory obligations, and enables informed decision-making by stakeholders.

---

# Communication Objectives

* Coordinate incident response activities.
* Share accurate and timely information.
* Protect sensitive incident information.
* Meet legal, contractual, and regulatory reporting requirements.
* Maintain a complete audit trail of communications.
* Reduce misinformation and unnecessary escalation.

---

# Communication Best Practices

* Use secure communication channels for incident-related discussions.
* Avoid sending sensitive information via unencrypted email or messaging platforms.
* Establish **out-of-band (OOB)** communication methods in case corporate email, collaboration tools, or VoIP services are compromised.
* Classify incident information according to the organization's data classification policy.
* Follow the **need-to-know** principle; share incident details only with authorized personnel.
* Encrypt emails and files containing sensitive information.
* Designate an Incident Communications Lead or Public Information Officer for external communications.
* Maintain accurate communication logs, including decisions, approvals, and notifications.
* Use standardized communication templates where possible.
* Ensure all public statements are reviewed by Legal and Corporate Communications before release.

---

# Internal Communication

## Before an Incident

Establish communication procedures as part of the Incident Response Plan.

### Incident Response Preparation

* Ensure all incident response team members understand the Incident Response Plan.
* Clearly define individual roles and responsibilities.
* Maintain an up-to-date contact list for:

  * Security Operations Center (SOC)
  * Incident Response (IR) Team
  * IT Operations
  * Network Team
  * Cloud Operations
  * Legal
  * Human Resources
  * Business Continuity Team
  * Executive Management
  * Public Relations / Corporate Communications

### Communication Planning

Develop a communication matrix that defines:

* Who should be notified
* Notification priority
* Communication methods
* Escalation paths
* Approval authorities
* Communication timelines

Prepare contact information including:

* Phone numbers
* Mobile numbers
* Email addresses
* Emergency contacts
* Backup contacts

---

## During an Incident

Communicate only verified information and avoid speculation.

Typical activities include:

* Notify the Incident Response team.
* Inform system and asset owners.
* Notify executive management based on incident severity.
* Coordinate containment and recovery activities.
* Conduct regular incident status meetings.
* Record significant decisions and actions.
* Escalate major incidents according to the incident response plan.

### Secure Communications

If internal systems are suspected to be compromised:

* Use out-of-band communication channels.
* Use mobile phones instead of corporate VoIP.
* Use secure collaboration platforms hosted outside the affected environment.
* Avoid using compromised email systems.
* Verify the identity of participants before sharing sensitive information.

---

## After an Incident

Following containment and recovery:

* Notify stakeholders that services have been restored.
* Conduct an incident review meeting.
* Prepare an After-Action Report (AAR).
* Document lessons learned.
* Update communication procedures based on findings.
* Archive communication records for compliance and auditing.

# External Communication

## Before an Incident

Establish relationships with external organizations before an incident occurs.

Identify primary Points of Contact (POCs) for:

* Law enforcement agencies
* National Computer Emergency Response Teams (CERTs)
* Cybersecurity authorities
* Cloud service providers
* Internet Service Providers (ISPs)
* Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs)
* Cyber insurance providers
* Legal counsel
* Third-party vendors

Document:

* Contact details
* Reporting procedures
* Escalation paths
* Reporting deadlines
* Required reporting formats

---

## During an Incident

Depending on the severity and applicable regulations, organizations may need to notify:

* Regulatory authorities
* Government cybersecurity agencies
* Customers
* Business partners
* Cloud service providers
* Cyber insurance providers
* Law enforcement

Provide verified information, including:

* Incident summary
* Date and time of detection
* Current incident status
* Affected systems and services
* Business impact
* Known Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
* Containment measures
* Recovery status
* Planned remediation activities

Continue providing updates as new information becomes available.

Typical updates may include:

* Expanded or reduced incident scope
* Updated timeline of events
* Newly discovered IOCs
* Changes in attacker tactics or behavior
* Updated impact assessment
* Progress toward containment
* Progress toward eradication
* Recovery milestones
* Estimated recovery completion time

---

## After an Incident

After the incident has been resolved:

* Submit final reports to regulators, where required.
* Notify affected customers if personal or sensitive information was compromised.
* Fulfill contractual notification obligations.
* Share relevant threat intelligence with trusted information-sharing communities, where appropriate.
* Conduct post-incident briefings with stakeholders.
* Publish approved public statements when necessary.
* Complete all required compliance reporting.

---

# Regulatory and Legal Considerations

Organizations should understand and comply with applicable reporting obligations, including:

* National cybersecurity regulations
* Privacy and data protection laws
* Industry-specific regulations
* Contractual reporting requirements
* Cyber insurance notification requirements

Examples include:

* GDPR (European Union)
* HIPAA (United States)
* PCI DSS
* NIS2 Directive
* SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rules
* National CERT reporting requirements

Always involve Legal and Compliance teams before making external disclosures.

# Communication Channels

| Channel                      | Typical Use                    | Notes                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Secure Email                 | Routine incident communication | Encrypt sensitive information.                         |
| Microsoft Teams / Slack      | Internal collaboration         | Use only if the environment is not compromised.        |
| Phone Calls                  | Urgent coordination            | Suitable for high-priority incidents.                  |
| Out-of-Band Communication    | Critical incidents             | Use when corporate systems cannot be trusted.          |
| Video Conference             | Executive briefings            | Verify participant identities.                         |

---

# Information That Should Not Be Shared

Avoid disclosing the following unless authorized:

* Investigation details
* Sensitive forensic evidence
* Security tool configurations
* Vulnerability details before remediation
* User credentials
* Encryption keys
* Internal network architecture
* Personally identifiable information (PII)
* Confidential business information

---

# Best Practices

* Develop a formal Incident Communication Plan.
* Define communication responsibilities before an incident occurs.
* Maintain updated contact lists.
* Use encrypted communication whenever possible.
* Establish out-of-band communication procedures.
* Communicate only verified facts.
* Keep executive leadership informed throughout the incident.
* Maintain detailed communication logs for auditing and legal purposes.
* Coordinate all external communications through authorized spokespersons.
* Review and improve communication procedures after every incident.
