Threat assessment is the practice of determining the credibility and seriousness of a potential threat, as well as the probability that the threat will become a reality.

Threat assessment aims to interrupt people on the way to perform cyber-attack.

## How to Assess the likelihood and impact of potential cyber threat?
To assess the likelihood and impact of potential cyber threats, organizations can consider the following factors:

### Likelihood
•	Exploitability: How easy is it for an attacker to exploit the vulnerability?
•	Motivation: Is there a motivation for an attacker to exploit the vulnerability? For example, if the vulnerability could be exploited to steal sensitive data or disrupt critical operations, then it is more likely to be exploited.
•	Access: Does an attacker have access to the system or network where the vulnerability is present?
•	Skills and resources: Does an attacker have the skills and resources necessary to exploit the vulnerability?

### Impact
•	Confidentiality: Would exploitation of the vulnerability result in the loss of confidentiality of sensitive data?
•	Integrity: Would exploitation of the vulnerability result in the modification of data or systems in an unauthorized way?
•	Availability: Would exploitation of the vulnerability result in the denial of access to data or systems?
•	Financial: Would exploitation of the vulnerability result in financial losses, such as through theft or disruption of operations?
•	Reputation: Would exploitation of the vulnerability damage the organization's reputation?

## Tools and techniques to assess the likelihood and impact of potential cyber threats:
•	Threat intelligence: Threat intelligence can provide information about the latest threats and attack vectors, as well as the motivations and capabilities of attackers.
•	Vulnerability scanning: Vulnerability scanners can identify vulnerabilities in IT systems and networks.
•	Penetration testing: Penetration testers simulate attacks to identify security vulnerabilities and assess the organization's security posture.
•	Risk assessment tools: Risk assessment tools can help organizations to assess the likelihood and impact of risks, and to prioritize risks based on their severity.

By considering the factors listed above and using the tools and techniques available, organizations can develop a better understanding of the likelihood and impact of potential cyber threats. This information can then be used to make informed decisions about security investments and to develop remediation plans to reduce the risks.

Here are some examples of how to assess the likelihood and impact of potential cyber threats:
•	Likelihood: A new zero-day vulnerability is discovered in a popular web browser. The vulnerability is easy to exploit and could be used to steal sensitive data from users. There is a high motivation for attackers to exploit the vulnerability, as it could be used to target a large number of users. Additionally, attackers have access to the web browser, as it is installed on millions of devices around the world. Finally, attackers have the skills and resources necessary to exploit the vulnerability, as it is a relatively simple exploit.
•	Impact: The exploitation of the zero-day vulnerability could result in the loss of confidentiality of sensitive data, such as login credentials, credit card numbers, and personal information. This could lead to identity theft, fraud, and financial losses. Additionally, the exploitation of the vulnerability could be used to disrupt critical operations, such as online banking and e-commerce. This could lead to loss of revenue and damage to the organization's reputation.

Based on this assessment, organizations should prioritize remediating the zero-day vulnerability as soon as possible. This could involve patching the web browser, implementing workarounds, or blocking the exploit.
